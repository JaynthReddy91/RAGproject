# Calculus Integration Guide: Rules, Methods, and Solved Problems

Integration is a fundamental concept in calculus representing the area under a curve, accumulation of quantities, or the reverse operation of differentiation (antiderivative).

---

## 1. Types of Integrals

### A. Indefinite Integrals
An indefinite integral represents a family of functions (antiderivatives) and always includes a constant of integration, $C$.
$$\int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + C \quad \text{where} \quad F'(x) = f(x)$$

### B. Definite Integrals
A definite integral has upper and lower limits ($a$ and $b$) and represents the net signed area bounded by the curve $f(x)$, the x-axis, and the vertical lines $x = a$ and $x = b$.
$$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = [F(x)]_{a}^{b} = F(b) - F(a) \quad \text{(Fundamental Theorem of Calculus)}$$

---

## 2. Fundamental Rules of Integration

* **Constant Multiple Rule**: $\int k \cdot f(x) \, dx = k \int f(x) \, dx$
* **Sum and Difference Rule**: $\int [f(x) \pm g(x)] \, dx = \int f(x) \, dx \pm \int g(x) \, dx$
* **Power Rule**: $\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (\text{for } n \neq -1)$
* **Logarithmic Form**: $\int \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \ln|x| + C$

## 4. Advanced Integration Methods

### A. Integration by Substitution (U-Substitution)
Used when the integrand contains a function and its derivative.
* **Method**:
  1. Identify a part of the integrand to set as $u = g(x)$.
  2. Find $du = g'(x) \, dx$.
  3. Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral, evaluate, and substitute back.
* **Example**: Evaluate $\int 2x \cos(x^2) \, dx$.
  - Let $u = x^2$, then $du = 2x \, dx$.
  - The integral becomes: $\int \cos(u) \, du = \sin(u) + C$.
  - Substitute back: $\sin(x^2) + C$.

### B. Integration by Parts
Based on the product rule of differentiation: $\int u \, dv = u v - \int v \, du$.
* **LIATE Rule** for choosing $u$ (in order of priority):
  1. **L** - Logarithmic functions (e.g., $\ln(x)$)
  2. **I** - Inverse trigonometric functions (e.g., $\arctan(x)$)
  3. **A** - Algebraic functions (e.g., $x^2$, $3x$)
  4. **T** - Trigonometric functions (e.g., $\sin(x)$)
  5. **E** - Exponential functions (e.g., $e^x$)

* **Example**: Evaluate $\int x \cos(x) \, dx$.
  - Using LIATE, choose $u = x$ (Algebraic) and $dv = \cos(x) \, dx$ (Trigonometric).
  - Calculate $du = 1 \, dx$ and $v = \sin(x)$.
  - Apply formula: $x \sin(x) - \int \sin(x) \, dx = x \sin(x) - (-\cos(x)) + C = x \sin(x) + \cos(x) + C$.

### C. Integration by Partial Fractions
Used to integrate rational expressions $\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}$ where the degree of the numerator is less than the denominator.
* **Method**:
  1. Factor the denominator $Q(x)$.
  2. Split into partial fraction terms: e.g., $\frac{1}{(x-a)(x-b)} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{x-b}$.
  3. Solve for coefficients $A$ and $B$, then integrate terms individually.

## 5. Solved Integration Questions

### Question 1: Find the integral of $\int x e^{x} \, dx$.
* **Type**: Integration by parts.
* **Step-by-step Solution**:
  1. Let $u = x$ and $dv = e^x \, dx$ (by LIATE).
  2. Then, $du = dx$ and $v = e^x$.
  3. Apply integration by parts formula:
     $$\int x e^x \, dx = x e^x - \int e^x \, dx$$
  4. Evaluate the remaining integral:
     $$\int x e^x \, dx = x e^x - e^x + C = e^x(x - 1) + C$$

### Question 2: Evaluate $\int \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \, dx$.
* **Type**: Integration by substitution.
* **Step-by-step Solution**:
  1. Let $u = \ln(x)$.
  2. Calculate the differential: $du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$.
  3. Substitute into the integral:
     $$\int u \, du = \frac{u^2}{2} + C$$
  4. Substitute back $u = \ln(x)$:
     $$\int \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \, dx = \frac{(\ln(x))^2}{2} + C$$

### Question 3: Find the definite integral $\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx$.
* **Type**: Definite integral.
* **Step-by-step Solution**:
  1. Find the antiderivative: $\int \sin(x) \, dx = -\cos(x)$.
  2. Apply the limits of integration:
     $$[-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi} = -\cos(\pi) - (-\cos(0))$$
  3. Substitute values ($\cos(\pi) = -1$, $\cos(0) = 1$):
     $$-(-1) - (-1) = 1 + 1 = 2$$
  4. The definite integral is $2$.
